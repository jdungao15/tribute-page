Dr. Jose Rizal

Jose Rizal was a national hero of the Philippines. He was a brilliant scholar, a talented writer, and a dedicated patriot.

Here's a timeline of Dr.Jose Rizal

  • 1861Born in Calamba, Laguna, Philippines
  • 1872Rizal's father, Francisco Mercado Rizal, was exiled to Dapitan, Mindanao, for his alleged involvement in the Cavite Mutiny. This event had a profound impact on Rizal, and it later inspired him to write the novel Noli Me Tangere.
  • 1877Graduates from Ateneo Municipal de Manila with a degree in Philosophy and Letters
  • 1882Travels to Spain to study medicine
  • 1884Rizal transferred to the University of Heidelberg to study ophthalmology. He was a brilliant student, and he graduated with honors in 1886
  • 1886Rizal published the novel Noli Me Tangere, which was an immediate bestseller. The novel was critical of Spanish rule in the Philippines, and it led to Rizal being exiled to Dapitan
  • 1887Publishes the novel El Filibusterismo, which is even more critical of Spanish rule
  • 1888Returns to the Philippines in disguise
  • 1892Rizal founded the La Liga Filipina, a civic organization that advocated for reforms in the Philippines. The organization was quickly suppressed by the Spanish authorities, and Rizal was exiled to Dapitan
  • 1896Rizal returned to the Philippines to join the Philippine Revolution against Spain. He was captured by the Spanish authorities and executed in Manila.
